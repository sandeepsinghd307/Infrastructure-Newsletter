Introduction

The significant growth in infrastructure over the past decade has been a key propeller in the development of the country. The Indian government has completely transformed its approach and implementation strategy for infrastructure in the country, from a need-based model of development to an integrated long-term growth plan aimed at catering to the needs of "New India". In the last ten years, India has invested over USD 1.2 Tn in building infrastructure. The strategy of the country had been to build core infrastructure for easing logistical movement, increasing energy capacities to ensure high quality uninterrupted power supply to businesses and invest in connectivity to ensure that the 1.4 Bn people are connected to each other through mobile connections and high-speed internet. The focus is on creating world class infrastructure in a holistic and integrated manner while promoting innovative and accommodating models for Infrastructure finance.

Sector In Focus - Renewable Energy

India's renewable energy installed capacity increased 286% in the last 7.5 years. India aims to spearhead decarbonization efforts through International Solar Alliance, One Sun-One World-One Grid and become a global green hydrogen exporter. Hon'ble Prime Minister's Panchamrit pledge of 50% share of renewables by 2030 will ensure that 6% YoY growth in demand is met through low tariffs while having a low carbon footprint. At the recent COP-27 in Egypt, India laid out the roadmap to realize the 2070 climate neutral target - Long-Term Low Emissions and Development Strategies (LT-LEDS)

National Green Hydrogen Mission (NGHM) with an outlay of INR 19,700 Cr will help in the decarbonization efforts, reduce dependency on fossil fuel imports and help green hard-to-abate sectors. The mission aims to make India a global hub for producing, using, and exporting green hydrogen and green ammonia.

The Prime Minister launched the National Portal for Rooftop Solar giving a fillip to rooftop solar installations. Estimated to benefit 10 Lakh households through a single window portal, it is another measure after the Open Access rules to generate demand for MSME solar generators and cater to the C&I consumers. The monsoon session witnessed the Electricity (Amendment) Bill 2022 and Energy Conversation (Amendment) Bill 2022 introduced by the Hon'ble Union Minister of Power and New & Renewable Energy, Shri R.K. Singh, with the latter being passed. These policy measures set the foundation for a renewables revolution across solar, wind (offshore and onshore), green hydrogen and battery infrastructure in India.
